The 70% solution indicates that it contains 70% of the active chemical compound (NH4)HSO3, with the rest being water. It is essential to handle Ammonium Bisulphite with care, as it is an irritant to the skin, eyes, and respiratory system. Proper safety precautions and handling procedures should be followed when using this chemical.

  • Paper and Pulp Industry: ABS is employed in the bleaching process of pulp to remove lignin and other unwanted substances.

Ammonium Bisulphite - Food Grade

Ammonium Bisulphite used in the food and beverage industry must meet specific purity and safety standards to be considered food grade. Food-grade ABS should not contain any harmful impurities that could be detrimental to human health when used as a food additive. It's important to follow the guidelines and regulations set by food authorities and regulatory agencies when using Ammonium Bisulphite in food applications. Manufacturers and food processors should comply with all applicable food safety regulations and good manufacturing practices.

Application

Ammonium Bisulphite (ABS) finds applications in several industries, mainly due to its ability to release sulfur dioxide when exposed to air. Its primary uses include:

  • Fruit and Vegetable Products: It is used in canned fruits and vegetables to preserve their color and flavor.
  • Wine and Beer:ABS is used in the production of wine and beer to prevent oxidation and maintain their freshness.
